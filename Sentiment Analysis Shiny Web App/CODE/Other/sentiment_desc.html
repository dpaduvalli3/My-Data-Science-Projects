About Sentiment Analysis

Sentiment analysis, a subfield of natural language processing (NLP), refers to the automated extraction of feeling or opinion from a text document. It is most popularly used for analyzing online reviews.

In order to quantitatively predict the sentiment of each token, we have used the following R dictionaries:

1.AFINN: assigns words with a score that runs between -5 and 5, with negative scores indicating negative sentiment and positive scores indicating positive sentiment.

2.Bing: categorizes words in a binary fashion into positive and negative categories (positive/negative).

3.NRC: assigns emotions for each tokenized word (ex: fear, happy).
